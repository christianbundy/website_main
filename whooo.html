whooo we are -
about the team

On a page like this, it is traditional to identify team members, highlight their respective backgrounds, & generally tout their expertise.

This is useful in determining the competence, trustworthiness, and qualifications of a project team.

Unfortunately, current political reality also turns such identity disclosures into a tempting attack surface for rogue state agencies worldwide.


In recent years, information activists - Julian Assange, Jacob Applebaum, Barrett Brown, Chelsea Manning, Edward Snowden, Thomas Drake, Glenn Greenwald, and countless others - have faced harrassment, persecution, threats of violence, and economic sabotage as a direct result of their work for free expression, online privacy, and spy-agency accountability.


As such, our team has made the difficult choice to remain pseudanonymous at this point in time.

This decision is taken not to protect ourselves, but rather to ensure a successful long-term outlook for cryptostorm's approach to decentralised, well-provisioned network security service.


We understand that our decision may cause some people to question whether our project can be trusted; we respect those concerns.

If this is the case for you, we regret that we cannot be of service.

It is our hope that our tangible work with cryptostorm - the service, the code, the supporting documentation & discussions - speaks to who we are and what we stand for.


No comment will be offered with respect to the individual members of the cryptostorm team, neither to confirm membership nor to deny assumptions of membership.

We ask that members of the media respect our request to attribute comments either to given pseudonyms, or simply to "cryptostorm" when quoting our work.


We have nothing to hide, and yet must hide ourselves due to the work we do in providing structurally anonymous network privacy service.

This is the world in which we live.

It is a damned shame - not even Orwell himself foresaw such necessary circumlocutions.

As a team, we genuinely look forward to a future in which such decisions are no longer necessary.
